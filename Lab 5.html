The following dataset that we will be examining are measurements in order to identify Higgs bosons at high transverse momenta, which decay to bottom-quark pairs. These measurements were made at the Large Hadron Collider.

This is an extension of the ATLAS experiment, of proton-proton collisions, to heavy new particles which may have decay chains that include Higgs bosons. The large mass-splitting between these heavier particles and their decay products results in high-momenta Higgs boson. Due to high-momenta, the decay products come off collimated (or in other words, coming off parallel to each other).

The particle collisions occur at a center-of-mass energy of 13 TeV. The ATLAS detector covers nearly the entire solid angle around the collision, with an inner tracking detector surrounded by a thin superconducting solenoid electromagnetic and hadronic calorimeter, and a muon spectrometer incorporating three large superconducting toroid magnets.

There are 14 different measurements in our dataset. These are $\rho$t, $\eta$, $\phi$, m, EE2, EE3, d2, angularity, $\tau$1, $\tau$2, $\tau$3, $\tau$21, $\tau$32, KtR:

$\rho$t represents the transverse momentum of the particle.
$\eta$ represents the pseudorapidity. The angle between the axis which runs through the path of particles colliding, and the vertical axis.
$\phi$ represents the azimuthal angle, which is the angle between the axis which runs across the ATLAS detector and the vertical axis.
m is the invariant mass. The portiion of the total mass that is independent of the total motion of the particle.

EE2 and EE3 are the 2 and 3-point ECF ratios. The ratios generally represent how far the particles are within a jet. d2 is the 3-to-2-point ECF ratio, representing the r angularity

$\tau$1, $\tau$2, $\tau$3, $\tau$21 are the values which represent how likely the measured particles are a 1, 2, or 3 prong jet. The $\tau$21, $\tau$32 represent the ratios between these values; $\tau$21 is the ratio between $\tau$2 and $\tau$2
